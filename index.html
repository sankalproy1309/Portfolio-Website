MY JOURNEY

The Start: Falling in Love with Photography

It all began with a camera. I still remember the thrill of holding one for the first time, marveling at the ability to capture a fleeting moment and preserve it forever. Photography quickly became my escape, a way to express myself and tell stories without words. I was fascinated by how light, angles, and timing could turn ordinary scenes into extraordinary visuals.


My initial attempts were simple: snapping pictures of sunsets, flowers, and candid portraits of friends and family. As I explored this art form, I dove into online tutorials, learning about composition, exposure, and editing. Each photograph taught me something new, and soon, my passion for capturing still moments evolved into an interest in motion.


The Transition to Videography

The shift from photography to videography was natural. If a picture could tell a story, a video could tell a chapter. I started experimenting with short clips, combining them into montages with simple transitions and music. Videography introduced me to the concept of storytelling on a broader canvas.


As I improved, I began to document events, trips, and everyday life, turning mundane routines into cinematic sequences. I discovered how much impact a well-timed frame, a dramatic angle, or an emotional piece of background music could have on the audience. Videography wasn’t just about recording—it was about evoking emotions, and that realization pushed me toward video editing.


Embracing Video Editing

Editing became my playground. I found immense satisfaction in piecing together raw footage to create something meaningful and impactful. Adobe Premiere Pro and DaVinci Resolve became my tools of choice, and hours spent tinkering with transitions, color grading, and audio mixing felt more like play than work.


I honed my skills by editing travel vlogs, event recaps, and passion projects. Each project was an opportunity to experiment—whether it was mastering speed ramping, learning green-screen techniques, or creating seamless transitions. Editing transformed my creative process. It wasn’t just about shooting anymore; it was about crafting an experience.


Discovering a Love for Travel

As I delved deeper into videography, travel became an inseparable part of my journey. Capturing new landscapes, cultures, and experiences expanded my perspective. Every trip was a treasure trove of inspiration, filled with stories waiting to be told.


I traveled not just for the sake of exploring new places but also to create. I filmed bustling cityscapes, serene mountain ranges, and vibrant markets, blending my passion for storytelling with my love for adventure. Traveling taught me resilience, adaptability, and the importance of stepping out of my comfort zone—qualities that would later serve me well in my coding and entrepreneurial endeavors.


Stepping Into Coding

While my love for visual storytelling grew, I found myself drawn to the digital tools that powered creativity. I wanted to understand the backend of the platforms I used every day—how they worked, how they were built, and how I could create my own. This curiosity led me to coding.


Starting with HTML and CSS, I began building simple websites. The ability to bring ideas to life on a screen was exhilarating. I soon ventured into JavaScript, Python, and other programming languages, finding parallels between coding and the art forms I already loved. Both required creativity, problem-solving, and an eye for detail.


As I progressed, I merged my creative and technical skills, building websites that showcased my photography and videography, and experimenting with interactive storytelling. Coding wasn’t just a skill; it became a new medium for expression.


Entering the World of Entrepreneurship

Now, I stand at the intersection of creativity, technology, and business. My journey has come full circle as I step into the entrepreneurial world. I’m building platforms and products that blend my passions—tools that empower others to create, explore, and share.


Entrepreneurship is teaching me a whole new set of skills—strategic thinking, leadership, and perseverance. It’s challenging but deeply fulfilling to see my ideas take shape and make an impact. Each step, from photography to coding to entrepreneurship, feels like a natural progression, each passion building on the last.
